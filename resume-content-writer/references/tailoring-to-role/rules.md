# Tailoring to Role Rules

Rules for re-aiming resume content at one specific target job.

## Core Rules

### 1. Mirror the posting's terminology and title where truthful

Reuse the ad's exact words whenever they honestly fit; ATS prefers exact matches and screeners miss synonyms.

- Set the objective header to the posted job title.
- Use the posting's exact terms in Areas of Expertise and Experience.
- Keep keyword nouns in plain text at least once (parsers strip styling).

**Example**:
- Generic: `Objective: A challenging position in a growth-oriented company`
- Mirrored: `Objective: Outside Sales Representative` (matches the posted title)

### 2. Translate past work with crossover language

Restate your experience in the target field's vocabulary so its value registers — without changing the facts.

- Re-label people (patients/members/stakeholders), work (sell vs. consult, implementation vs. deployment), and products in the target's terms.
- Bridge unrelated roles (e.g., cabinetry → project management) by reframing, not inventing.

### 3. Lead with the most relevant experience

Put top placement and most detail on the experience that best proves the target role.

- If most recent = most relevant, lead with it; otherwise lead with whatever is most relevant.
- Order bullets within each role strongest-to-least against the posting.
- Front-load the top bullets under each role.

### 4. Demote and trim irrelevant material

Cut content and words that don't support the target goal ("conflict noise").

- Give older/less-relevant roles only a brief hook + a few top accomplishment bullets.
- Cap at ~4 positions or 10–15 years; list the rest title-only under "Additional Employment" (no dates).
- Remove details that don't help the target (e.g., retail/ops specifics when targeting outside sales).

### 5. Select and order the skills section to match the JD's priorities

Build Areas of Expertise from the posting, in the posting's priority order, then prove each in Experience.

- Use the posting's exact skill terms; keep ~9–21 bullets across 1–3 columns.
- Order top-to-bottom by what the posting emphasizes most.
- Don't list a skill you can't back up below with a real outcome.

### 6. Paraphrase the ad — don't plagiarize it

Mirror individual terms and titles, but write your own bullets; do not copy the ad's sentences verbatim.

- Keywords belong in two places: Areas of Expertise and Experience.
- Make terms flow organically inside result-first CAR bullets.

### 7. Never fabricate to match

Only use language and claims you can defend in an interview.

- If a line isn't provably yours, cut it.
- Never invent numbers, brands, or credentials to fit the posting.

## Guidelines

- For a vague posting, scan 3–5 similar postings for the same title and harvest common terms.
- Mine the posting for: the title to mirror, what the employer values, key skills/keywords, and crossover wording.
- Use the Outcome → Action → Scope/Tools shape; quantify where true; past tense; no "I"; power verbs.
- Read each bullet aloud once and cut anything that doesn't prove the target role.

## Exceptions

When a generic (non-tailored) resume is acceptable:

- **Job fairs**: A clean two-page Core for first contact; follow up with an OnTarget version.
- **Single-job/single-niche focus**: One strong resume can work, but still tweak for major postings.
- **Networking / references / social profiles**: Share a broadly consistent general resume; respond to real leads OnTarget.
