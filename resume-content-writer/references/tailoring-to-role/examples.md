# Tailoring to Role Examples

Before/after pairs showing how to re-aim resume content at a specific target job.

## Crossover Language: generic bullet → role-tailored bullet

### Pair 1 — Cabinetry experience → Project Management target

**Before (generic / source field):**
> Built custom kitchen cabinetry for residential clients, managing materials and timelines.

**After (crossover to PM terms):**
> Delivered 30+ custom build projects on schedule by scoping requirements, managing budgets and material procurement, and coordinating subcontractor timelines.

**Why it works**: Reframes "cabinetry" work as scoping, budget, procurement, and coordination — the target's vocabulary — without inventing new experience.

### Pair 2 — Retail/ops phrasing → Outside Sales target

**Before:**
> Oversaw store operations and staff scheduling for a high-volume retail location.

**After:**
> Grew location revenue 18% YoY by consultative selling to walk-in and repeat customers, then expanding the account base through referrals.

**Why it works**: Drops ops "conflict noise," mirrors sales language (consultative selling, account base), and leads with a quantified outcome.

### Pair 3 — Internal IT phrasing → Customer-facing "deployment" target

**Before:**
> Installed and configured software on internal company machines.

**After:**
> Led client-site deployments of the platform across 40+ accounts, cutting average onboarding time from 3 weeks to 9 days.

**Why it works**: Re-labels "install" as "deployment," "company machines" as "client-site / accounts" — matching how the posting names work and people — and adds a true metric.

## Reordering Experience for Relevance

### Before — strict reverse-chron, top role least relevant to target (Data Analyst)

> 1. Office Manager — Acme Corp (current)
> 2. Junior Data Analyst — DataCo
> 3. Statistics Tutor — State University

### After — relevant-first emphasis

> 1. Junior Data Analyst — DataCo *(expanded: hook + 6 result-first bullets, top placement of detail)*
> 2. Statistics Tutor — State University *(brief hook + 2 outcome bullets)*
> 3. Office Manager — Acme Corp *(title-only / one line; low relevance)*

**Why it works**: When most-recent ≠ most-relevant, energy and detail shift to the role that best proves the target; the off-target current role is demoted, not deleted.

## Skills Section Retargeted from Job A to Job B

Same candidate, same true skills — selected and reordered to each posting's priorities.

### Areas of Expertise — Job A (Project Manager)

> Project Scoping · Budget Management · Stakeholder Communication · Risk Mitigation · Vendor/Subcontractor Coordination · Scheduling · Quality Control

### Areas of Expertise — Job B (Operations Analyst)

> Process Improvement · Data Analysis · Budget Tracking · Vendor Management · Reporting & Dashboards · Cross-Functional Coordination · Quality Control

**Changes made**:
1. Reordered to put each posting's top-priority terms first.
2. Swapped in Job B's exact terms (Process Improvement, Reporting & Dashboards) drawn from that ad.
3. Kept only skills provable in Experience for each target; dropped non-matching ones.

## Over-Cloning Trap: stuffed vs. organic

### Before (keyword-stuffed — anti-pattern)

> CRM, Salesforce, CRM software, customer relationship management, CRM tools, pipeline, CRM pipeline management, CRM reporting.

**Problems**: Reads as spam, fails the human skim, repeats one concept, proves nothing.

### After (mirrored once, proven in context)

> Managed a 200-account sales pipeline in Salesforce (CRM), lifting customer retention 12% through targeted follow-up cadences.

**Why it works**: Mirrors the exact term once in plain text, embeds it in a result-first bullet, and proves the term drove an outcome.
