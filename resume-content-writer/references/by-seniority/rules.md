# Framing Achievements by Seniority Rules

Do/don't rules for tailoring resume content to a candidate's seniority level.

## New-Grad / Entry-Level Rules

### 1. Mine all experience, including non-paid

Internships, part-time/campus jobs, freelance, entrepreneurial ventures, and volunteer/leadership roles all count.

- List them under "Experience" in RC order, formatted like real jobs.
- Always include title, organization, city, and dates — even when unpaid (ATS needs this).

### 2. Lead with results, not duties

State the impact: efficiency gained, project led, top marks earned.

- Describe outcomes (results created, problems solved), not task lists.
- Quantify wherever possible (GPA 3.5+, %, $, counts, selection results).

### 3. Add a Knowledge, Skills & Training block

When experience is thin, place a 2-3 column keyword list directly under the summary.

- Source terms from coursework, syllabi, projects, and target job postings.
- This gives immediate keyword-matching power and seeds the Experience section.

### 4. Make unrelated work relatable

Reframe past roles to surface transferable skills (customer service, multitasking, problem-solving).

- Optionally restate the function as a title: "Customer Service Rep (Title: Cashier)".
- Map past tasks to keywords pulled from target postings.

### 5. Don't apologize for inexperience — and don't pad

- Don't skip the keyword/skills section assuming you have nothing to offer.
- Don't pad with hobbies, personal interests, or high school activities unless they directly support the target job.
- Don't list everything you've ever done; focus on what sells the current goal.

## Experienced / Mature Rules

### 1. Focus on the last ~10-15 years

That is where employers and ATS look first.

- Put older but valuable roles under "Key/Additional/Prior Experience" — titles, employers, locations in body text, dates omitted (or total years only).

### 2. Lead with scope and quantified impact

Open with a summary that hooks strengths to the target field; concentrate on the two most recent/relevant roles.

- Show "tower of strength": problems solved, costs cut, systems improved, fast adaptation.
- Older or unrelated experience does not get equal billing.

### 3. Don't age-date the resume

- Remove graduation years — what you studied matters more than when.
- Drop outdated standards: marital status, unrelated hobbies, decades of detail.
- Prove currency: recent certifications, software proficiency, continuing education, online collaboration.

### 4. Don't list everything; trim to target

- Cut content that doesn't support the current goal, however hard-won.
- Don't skip the summary — without it, a strong career reads as a flat list.
- Skip high school even if it is the only education completed.

## Guidelines

- **Education placement**: New grad / thin experience → education can lead. One+ year of full-time work → experience first, education below (unless degree drives a pivot).
- **Heading flexibility**: "Experience" or "Professional Experience" covers paid + unpaid; refine to the field ("Accounting Education") when pivoting.
- **Both levels**: Always use an RC format for clean parsing; study target job descriptions and frame around employer ROI.
- **Stepping down**: Treat a lower-level move as intentional strategy; lead with transferable skills + examples, and explain the "why" via networking, not the resume alone.

## Exceptions

- **Career change via new credentials (any level)**: List education before experience and tailor the heading to the target field.
- **Networking phase / re-entry / bridging industries**: A hybrid format may be used to spotlight transferable skills, even though RC is the application default.

## Quick Reference

| Dimension | New-grad | Experienced |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Evidence | Projects, internships, volunteer, part-time, GPA | Scope, leadership, sustained results |
| Section order | Education/projects can lead | Experience leads; trim education |
| Non-paid roles | Include, formatted like jobs | Fold into "Key/Additional Experience" |
| Dates | Include on all entries | Drop grad year; thin or omit old dates |
| Top risk | Apologizing / padding filler | Over-listing / age-dating |
| Quantify | GPA, %, selection results | $ saved, % improved, teams/budgets led |
| Currency proof | Course-based tools, project tech | Recent certs, software, continuing ed |
