# Framing Achievements by Seniority Examples

Resume bullets and snippets showing how framing changes across seniority levels. "Examples" = resume content.

## Same Experience, Two Seniority Frames

The underlying work (running customer transactions at a grocery store) framed for each level.

### Entry-level frame (lead with transferable skills, restate the title)

> **Customer Service Rep (Title: Cashier)** – ABC Grocery, City, ST | Dates
> - Managed high-volume customer transactions with speed and accuracy while promoting in-store loyalty programs; recognized as Employee of the Month 5X in 2 years.

**Why it works**: Reframes a non-target job around transferable skills (customer service, multitasking) and shows a quantified result instead of duties.

### Bad version (duties only — avoid at any level)

> **Cashier** – ABC Grocery, City, ST | Dates
> - Scanned items and processed payments.

**Problems**: Lists duties, no outcome, no transferable-skill signal, no metrics.

## New-Grad Achievement Built From a Class Project

> **Marketing, FT Student** – ABC University, City, ST | 1/2021-Present
> - **Marketing Campaigns:** Designed and presented a campaign for XYZ Company selected by ABC Ad Agency as the top project out of 24 submissions; collaborated with agency staff on a mock pitch presentation.
> - **Marketing Collateral:** Created press releases, websites, brochures, and social assets using Canva, Photoshop, and WordPress as part of advanced design coursework.

**Why it works**: Coursework is formatted like a job (title, org, city, dates) so ATS parses it; leads with a quantified, competitive result; embeds tool keywords.

## New-Grad Achievement Built From a Volunteer / Leadership Role

> **President (Marketing Responsibility)** – 123 Sorority, City, ST | 1/2023-Present
> - **Recruiting & Marketing Campaigns:** Partnered with volunteers on on- and off-campus recruiting that drove a 30% increase in pledges over the prior three years.
> - **Communications & Promotions:** Served as spokesperson to university administrators and secured approval for a fundraiser that raised $25,000 in one semester.

**Why it works**: Unpaid leadership counts as experience; quantifies impact (30%, $25,000) and shows scope and initiative.

## New-Grad Summary

> Visionary, high-energy marketing graduate recognized for creativity developing campaigns for real-world brands including XYZ Company and ABC Company. Campaign selected by an agency panel out of 24 presented by fellow competitors. Driven to success — attained 3.75 GPA in BS in Marketing.

**Why it works**: Authentic (grounded in real classroom/internship work), quantified (GPA, selection result), and targeted (field keywords). Keep to 3-5 lines in the top third.

## Senior Leadership / Scope Bullet ("Tower of Strength")

> Led a 12-person cross-functional team through a platform migration, cutting processing costs 28% and reducing incident resolution time from 6 hours to under 90 minutes while adapting the workflow to new compliance requirements.

**Why it works**: Leads with scope (team size, project), proves resilience through quantified results, and signals adaptability/currency — all within recent, relevant experience.

## Senior Career-Change Bullet (engineer → sales)

> Leveraged a strong technical background and communication skills to translate complex solutions for clients and support key business decisions.

**Why it works**: Foregrounds transferable strengths (leadership, communication, problem-solving) to bridge past experience to a new target, backed by an example rather than a label.

## Senior: Folding Old Roles Without Age-Dating

> **Prior Work History**
> - Operations Manager – DEF Manufacturing, City, ST
> - Production Supervisor – GHI Industries, City, ST

**Why it works**: Keeps older valuable roles searchable and as interview talking points (titles, employers, locations) while omitting dates — focus stays on the recent ~10-15 years and avoids revealing full career span.
