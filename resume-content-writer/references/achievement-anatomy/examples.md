# Achievement Anatomy Examples

Before/after rewrites turning duties into quantified, results-first bullets.

## Responsibility → Achievement (telling → selling)

| Before (tells a duty) | After (sells a result) |
|-----------------------|------------------------|
| Managed day-to-day business operations. | Turned around an underperforming center, lifting its rank from 23rd to 7th of 27 in under a year. |
| Handled inventory management. | Cut monthly inventory shrink from 20% to under 5%, saving ~$1,200/month. |
| Trained staff in selling techniques. | Boosted average ticket from $350 to $580 by training staff on platinum-tier upselling. |
| Sold memberships and products. | Reactivated lapsed members via outbound calls and open houses, upgrading 40% of clients to the premium plan. |

**Why the "after" wins**: each leads with the outcome, attaches a figure, and is unique to the candidate.

## Vague → Specific

| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| Significantly increased sales. | Increased quarterly sales 32%, hitting goal for the first time in 2 years. |
| Supervised a large team. | Supervised a staff of 12 (10 FT, 2 PT) plus an assistant manager. |
| Improved customer satisfaction a lot. | Raised customer satisfaction from 66% to 91% in two quarters. |
| Reduced costs substantially. | Reduced middle-management costs by $48K through staff restructuring. |

## Say It With Numbers (#)

Use for counts, scale, rank, tenure, headcount.

- Recruited 14 staff members in 6 months, fully closing a chronic understaffing gap.
- Sold 3,200 units in Q4, ranking 1st in sales among 45 reps company-wide.

## Say It With Percentages (%)

Use for relative change, rate, goal attainment, ranking.

- Resolved client-relations backlog, increasing customer satisfaction 25%.
- Streamlined the monthly closeout procedure, cutting hours spent on the task by 40%.

## Say It With Dollar Amounts ($)

Use for revenue, savings, budget, cost cuts — the heaviest-hitting lever.

- Designed a referral program that earned $86K in new annual membership revenue.
- Opened a new location $30K under projected budget while hitting the launch deadline.

## Full CAR Walkthrough

### Before (raw responsibility note)

> Trained staff in selling and customer-service techniques; sold memberships and products.

### After (CAR-built bullet)

> **Lifted average ticket 66% ($350 → $580) and began meeting quarterly sales goals** by training staff on platinum-tier upselling with scripts, role-plays, and weekly incentives.

### Changes Made

1. **Led with the result** (66% lift, goals met) instead of the task (training).
2. **Quantified** with both a percentage and a dollar before/after.
3. **Compressed the action** (how) into a trailing clause, keeping one accomplishment per bullet.
4. **Removed vague verbs** ("trained...sold") in favor of an impact-first verb ("Lifted").
