# Wow Words Knowledge

Core concepts for the power verbs and language that make resume bullets punch.

## Overview

*Wow words* are intentional word choices that make a resume clearer, stronger, and more persuasive at a glance. On a resume, words do the heavy lifting: a strong action verb turns a flat "responsible for" line into an energetic, results-first bullet while keeping keywords in view. This category covers the action-verb half of wow words, organized for quick lookup.

## Key Concepts

### Wow words

**Definition**: The intentional word choices that make a resume clearer, stronger, and more persuasive at a glance.

They come in two flavors, both of which matter together:

- **Action verbs** (this category's focus) bring stories to life and make bullets dynamic and outcome-driven (e.g., *revived, streamlined, negotiated, engineered*).
- **Keywords** are usually nouns that match search terms and requirements (e.g., *CRM, OSHA compliance, Python, revenue operations*).

**Key points**:
- Keywords help you get *found* and pass screens; action verbs help you get *chosen*.
- Use both together for the biggest impact.

### Action verbs (wow verbs)

**Definition**: Lively, energetic verbs that communicate abilities and accomplishments with power and substance.

They replace flat "responsible for ..." statements with dynamic, outcome-driven language.

**Key points**:
- Lead with an outcome, pick a precise verb, then anchor it with keyword nouns.
- Choose verbs that are authentic for you — don't claim what you didn't do.
- Avoid reusing the same verb; strive for original use in each bullet.

### Functional verb categories

**Definition**: Action verbs grouped by the job-posting role/function they best describe, so you can match the verb to the target job.

The chapter organizes recommended wow verbs into nine functional categories. Looking across the lists, you can find verbs that fit almost any role.

**Key points**:
- Pick verbs from the category that matches the target function.
- A single accomplishment can often be framed with verbs from more than one category — choose the framing that best fits the job description.

## Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Wow words | Intentional word choices that make a resume stronger at a glance |
| Action verb / wow verb | Energetic verb that opens a bullet with power (revived, streamlined) |
| Keyword | Usually a noun that mirrors job-posting search terms (CRM, Python) |
| Outcome-first | Ordering a bullet to lead with the result, then action, then challenge |
| CAR story | Challenge–Action–Result structure for accomplishment bullets |

## How It Relates To

- **Keywords / job-description matching**: Action verbs pair with keyword nouns; verbs get you chosen, keywords get you found.
- **CAR stories (Challenge–Action–Result)**: Outcome-first bullets reorder a CAR story to lead with the result.
- **Resume polishing / grammar**: Active verbs + consistent past tense + short, clear lines = winning content.

## Common Misconceptions

- **Myth**: A thesaurus full of fancy words makes a resume stronger.
  **Reality**: You don't need a thesaurus — you need precise, authentic action verbs paired with keywords.

- **Myth**: Keywords alone are enough to win the job.
  **Reality**: Keywords pass the screen; action verbs are what persuade the human to choose you.

- **Myth**: Listing duties ("responsible for ...") shows your value.
  **Reality**: Duty lists are flat; outcome-first bullets led by strong verbs show value.
