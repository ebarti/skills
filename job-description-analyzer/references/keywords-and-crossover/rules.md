# Keywords & Crossover Rules

Rules for extracting ATS keywords from a JD and mapping them to the candidate's truthful vocabulary.

## Core Rules

### 1. Extract the exact high-value keyword phrases

Pull titles, tools, skills, certifications/licenses, and valued outcomes verbatim.

- Cover all five families: **title**, **skills/tools**, **requirements**, **certs/licenses**, **outcomes** (quota, retention, uptime).
- Group every keyword as **must-have** vs. **nice-to-have**.
- For each, note a common synonym/variant (e.g., account management / client success) — but keep the JD's term as primary.

**Example**:
> Bad: skills: ["good with customers", "tech tools"]
> Good: must_have: ["Salesforce", "B2B sales", "quota attainment"]; nice_to_have: ["HubSpot", "SQL"]

### 2. Prefer the JD's exact phrasing for hard requirements

For must-haves, mirror the posting's words verbatim — software prefers exact matches and screeners may miss synonyms.

- Use the exact term as written ("CRM" not "customer database tools" if the ad says CRM).
- Reserve synonyms/variants as secondary metadata, not replacements.

### 3. Build truthful crossover mappings only

Create a `their_term → your_equivalent` row **only where the candidate genuinely has the equivalent**.

- One row per requirement: their term → proof/source → suggested wording.
- Leave gaps honest — not every JD term gets a mapping.
- Only map language the candidate can **defend in an interview**.

**Example**:
> Bad: "deployment" → "deployment" (no real equivalent; faked)
> Good: "deployment" → "rollout/implementation" (candidate ran installs)

### 4. Mirror in the right places, naturally

Place keywords in two zones: **Areas of Expertise** (list) and **Experience** (proof bullets).

- Make terms flow organically; never stack or dump them.
- Prove usage, not just presence — tie the term to an outcome.

### 5. Never stuff or fabricate

Do not copy the ad wholesale, repeat terms unnaturally, or claim skills the candidate lacks.

- No keyword walls or hidden/padded term lists.
- No term the candidate can't back up with experience.

## How Much to Mirror vs. Paraphrase

| Element | Mirror exactly | Paraphrase / crossover |
|---------|---------------|------------------------|
| Job title (objective header) | Yes | — |
| Hard skills, tools, certs | Yes (verbatim) | Add variant as metadata only |
| Required outcomes/metrics | Yes (the metric term) | — |
| Soft/described duties | When truthful | Crossover into your terms |
| Experience the candidate has in another field's words | — | Translate via crossover map |

Rule of thumb: **mirror hard requirements, crossover-translate soft/adjacent experience.**

## Guidelines

- If the JD is vague, scan 3–5 similar postings for the same title and extract common terms.
- Note how the employer labels **people** (patients/members/stakeholders), **work** (sell vs. consult), and **products** — mirror those labels.
- Flag licenses/certs separately; they are often hard gates.
- Fit signal: apply when the candidate meets most must-haves and ~70–80% of nice-to-haves with adjacent/crossover proof.

## Exceptions

- **Generic resume contexts** (job fairs, single-niche focus, networking): a non-tailored core is acceptable, so deep keyword mirroring is optional — still tweak for major postings.
- **No genuine equivalent**: omit the crossover row entirely rather than force a weak or false mapping.

## Quick Reference

| Rule | Do | Don't |
|------|----|-------|
| Extract | Pull exact title/tool/skill/cert/outcome terms, split must/nice | Paraphrase hard requirements away |
| Hard requirements | Mirror verbatim | Substitute a synonym |
| Crossover map | Map only truthful equivalents | Invent equivalents to fill gaps |
| Placement | Areas of Expertise + Experience, flowing | Stack/dump keywords |
| Integrity | Use only interview-defendable terms | Stuff or fabricate |
