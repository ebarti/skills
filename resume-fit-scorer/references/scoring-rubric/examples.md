# Scoring Rubric Examples

Fully worked resume-vs-JD scorings: per-dimension scores with one-line justifications, the weighted overall, and the verdict band. Resumes are abbreviated to the load-bearing lines.

---

## Example 1 — Weak (untargeted, duty-listing)

**JD (Data Analyst):** "SQL, Python, dashboards (Tableau/Looker), A/B testing, stakeholder reporting. 3+ yrs."

**Resume (excerpt):**
> Objective: Seeking a challenging role offering growth.
> Office Coordinator, 2019–present
> - Responsible for daily operations and reporting.
> - Worked on various spreadsheets and ad-hoc analysis.
> - Helped teams with data when needed.

**Per-dimension scores:**

| Dim | W | Score | Justification |
|-----|---|-------|---------------|
| JD Match | 30% | 2 | None of SQL/Python/Tableau/A-B testing present; only vague "spreadsheets" |
| Achievement | 25% | 2 | Pure duties ("responsible for", "worked on"); zero metrics |
| Targeting | 15% | 2 | Me-centric objective; aimed at no role; off-target title |
| ATS | 10% | 6 | RC-ish, standard-ish headings; parsable but has "Objective" label |
| Red Flags | 10% | 6 | "Objective" label + vague filler (−); no typos noted |
| Language | 10% | 3 | "Responsible for", passive, no power verbs/keyword nouns |

**Overall:** 2×.30 + 2×.25 + 2×.15 + 6×.10 + 6×.10 + 3×.10 = 0.60+0.50+0.30+0.60+0.60+0.30 = **2.9 → 3–4 Weak**

**Critique:** Missing every must-have (SQL, Python, Tableau, A/B testing) — fatal for JD Match. No quantified results. Rewrite bullets as CAR with metrics, add the actual tools, and replace the objective with a "Data Analyst" header. Currently auto-reject territory.

## Example 2 — OK-but-not-competitive

**JD (Backend Engineer):** "Java, Spring Boot, microservices, AWS, REST APIs, CI/CD. 4+ yrs."

**Resume (excerpt):**
> Software Engineer, 2020–present
> - Built REST APIs in Java/Spring Boot serving internal tools.
> - Reduced API latency 30% by adding caching.
> - Deployed services to AWS; maintained Jenkins pipelines.
> - Collaborated on microservices migration.

**Per-dimension scores:**

| Dim | W | Score | Justification |
|-----|---|-------|---------------|
| JD Match | 30% | 7 | Java, Spring Boot, AWS, REST, CI/CD present; microservices only "collaborated on" |
| Achievement | 25% | 5 | One strong metric (30% latency); rest are duties without numbers |
| Targeting | 15% | 5 | No objective header tying to "Backend Engineer"; generic title |
| ATS | 10% | 8 | RC, standard headings, clean, parsable |
| Red Flags | 10% | 9 | No typos/gaps/clutter observed |
| Language | 10% | 7 | Decent verbs ("Built", "Reduced"); "Collaborated on" is weak |

**Overall:** 7×.30 + 5×.25 + 5×.15 + 8×.10 + 9×.10 + 7×.10 = 2.10+1.25+0.75+0.80+0.90+0.70 = **6.5 → 5–6 OK**

**Critique:** Good keyword coverage but only one quantified win — Achievement (25%) drags it down. Add metrics to the AWS and microservices bullets (scale, throughput, # services), add a "Backend Engineer" header, and upgrade "Collaborated on" to an ownership verb. Solid but not yet competitive.

## Example 3 — Strong

**JD (Product Marketing Manager):** "Go-to-market strategy, positioning, sales enablement, cross-functional leadership, B2B SaaS. 5+ yrs."

**Resume (excerpt):**
> Product Marketing Manager — B2B SaaS
> Summary: GTM and positioning lead driving cross-functional launches.
> - Led GTM for 3 product launches, generating $4.2M new-pipeline in year one.
> - Repositioned flagship product; lifted win rate 18% across 120 reps.
> - Built sales-enablement kit adopted by 100% of the field team.
> - Drove cross-functional launch team (product, sales, success).

**Per-dimension scores:**

| Dim | W | Score | Justification |
|-----|---|-------|---------------|
| JD Match | 30% | 9 | GTM, positioning, sales enablement, cross-functional, B2B SaaS all present, in JD's language, up top |
| Achievement | 25% | 9 | Every bullet CAR + quantified ($4.2M, 18%, 100%, 3 launches) |
| Targeting | 15% | 9 | Header mirrors exact title; one GTM theme throughout |
| ATS | 10% | 8 | RC, standard headings, clean; assume PDF |
| Red Flags | 10% | 10 | No typos/gaps/clutter/bias |
| Language | 10% | 8 | Power verbs ("Led", "Repositioned", "Drove") + keyword nouns; tight |

**Overall:** 9×.30 + 9×.25 + 9×.15 + 8×.10 + 10×.10 + 8×.10 = 2.70+2.25+1.35+0.80+1.00+0.80 = **8.9 → 7–8 Strong**

**Critique:** Precise JD alignment with quantified impact on every bullet — exactly what wins screens. To reach Excellent (9–10): confirm PDF/parsable file and add the one missing scope detail (team size led). Minor fixes only.

---

## Pattern Recap

| Signal in resume | Pushes toward |
|------------------|---------------|
| Missing must-haves, duty lists, me-centric objective | Trash / Weak (0–4) |
| Keywords present but few metrics, generic header | OK (5–6) |
| Must-haves + quantified CAR + clean + tailored | Strong (7–8) |
| All of the above + zero flags + confirmed ATS-clean | Excellent (9–10) |
