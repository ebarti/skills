# Scoring Rubric Knowledge

Core definitions for the 0–10 resume-fit score: the six dimensions, their weights, and the verdict bands. JobHunter uses this to score a resume against a specific job description (JD).

## Overview

A resume is scored on six weighted dimensions, each rated 0–10. The weighted average produces an overall 0–10 score, which maps to a verdict band. The rubric is synthesized from the book's two checklists (Ch15 "Ten Ways to Improve Your Resume", Ch18 "Ten-Point Resume Checklist"): every dimension is justified by specific checklist items so scores are defensible, not vibes.

## The Six Dimensions

### 1. JD Match — 30% (heaviest)

**Measures**: How completely the resume covers the JD's stated must-haves and nice-to-haves (skills, tools, scope, education) using the role's own language, concentrated in the top third.

**Book basis**: Ch18 "Targeting Qualifications for a Match" (qualifications mirror the posting's must-haves/nice-to-haves in the top third; use the role's language so screeners and software see a direct match). Ch15 "Matching Your Resume to the Job" (read the posting like a checklist; back every key term with a proof point). The single biggest predictor of getting a real look — hence the heaviest weight.

### 2. Achievement Strength — 25%

**Measures**: Whether bullets prove outcomes with quantified, action-led results (CAR: Challenge–Action–Result), not recited duties.

**Book basis**: Ch18 "Highlighting Accomplishments and Skills" (pair every called-out skill with a concrete win; CAR with a number — %, $, time, volume, ranking; if you can't quantify, qualify scope/frequency/speed). Ch15 "Selling, Not Telling" + "Showing Off Your Value" (replace "responsible for" with CAR; name the scale — "Top 5 of 120 reps", "Cut defects 32%", "$4.2M P&L"). Proof is what gets you chosen once you're found.

### 3. Targeting & Focus — 15%

**Measures**: Whether the resume is tailored to THIS role with one unifying theme, the right things featured up top, and off-target content trimmed.

**Book basis**: Ch18 "Sharpening Focus" (one unifying theme; clear objective header; trim off-target details) + "Funneling Content to the Goal". Ch15 "Reaching Out with Strength" (aim every section at the job you want), "Trashing Wimpy Objectives" (state the target plainly; mirror the job title), and the 5% rule (customize at least the top 5% per posting).

### 4. ATS Parseability — 10%

**Measures**: Whether structure, format, and file type are machine-readable: reverse-chronological, standard headings, consistent dates, clean layout, parsable file.

**Book basis**: Ch18 "Selecting the Right Format and Style" (reverse-chronological for online apps; standard headings — Summary, Experience, Education, Skills; consistent dates; clean layout). Ch15 "Delivering the Right File, Every Time" (submit .docx or PDF; standard labels and body text for upload parsing accuracy).

### 5. Red Flags — 10%

**Measures**: Presence of disqualifiers and distractions: typos, unexplained gaps, job-hopping, clutter (Objective label, "References available", personal IDs, prep dates, high school), bias-inviting/irrelevant details, AI slop. Starts at 10; each flag deducts.

**Book basis**: Ch18 "Proofreading, then Proofreading Again" (typos are instant deal-breakers) + "Navigating Sticky Points Strategically" (address gaps; group short contracts; drop very old/off-target roles). Ch15 "Erasing What Doesn't Belong" (remove "References available", SSN/license, prep dates, phone numbers, high school, roles >10–15 yrs unless crucial; avoid bias-inviting details).

### 6. Language — 10%

**Measures**: Concision, power verbs, keyword nouns, active voice, and consistency in tense/jargon.

**Book basis**: Ch18 "Polishing Language and Power Expressions" (tighten every word; strong action verbs; cut filler/cliches; active voice without "I"; consistent tense/jargon; translate acronyms). Ch15 "Discovering the Art of Lost Articles" (drop a/an/the and "I"; cut "responsible for"), "Using Bulleted Style" (bullets <3 lines, white space), "Making Sure Your Words Work Together" (verbs energize + nouns make you findable).

| 3 | Targeting & Focus | 15% | Tailored, one theme, right things up top |
| 4 | ATS Parseability | 10% | RC format, standard headings, parsable file |
| 5 | Red Flags | 10% | Absence of typos/gaps/clutter/bias/AI slop |
| 6 | Language | 10% | Concision, power verbs, keyword nouns, consistency |
| — | **Total** | **100%** | — |

## 0–10 Verdict Bands

| Band | Label | Verdict |
|------|-------|---------|
| 0–2 | **Trash** | Irrelevant to the JD or major red flags; will be auto-rejected |
| 3–4 | **Weak** | Some relevance but generic, few metrics, missing must-haves |
| 5–6 | **OK** | Basics covered, partial must-have coverage, some metrics; not competitive |
| 7–8 | **Strong** | Strong JD alignment, quantified achievements, clean; minor fixes |
| 9–10 | **Excellent** | Precise JD match, compelling quantified impact, zero red flags, ATS-clean |
