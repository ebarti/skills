# JD-Match Rules

Rules for assessing how well a resume matches a job description and whether it survives ATS parsing plus a recruiter's glance. Apply these when scoring the jd-match dimension.

## Core Rules

### 1. Measure must-have coverage (truthfully)

Count how many of the JD's must-have requirements (skills, years, certifications, tools) actually appear in the resume — backed by evidence.

- Extract the JD's must-haves first; treat them as the checklist.
- A must-have "counts" only if it is present AND supported (claimed in skills *and* proven in Experience).
- Reward truthful coverage; do not reward stuffed or unsupported keywords.
- **Penalize**: each missing must-have; skills listed but never substantiated.

### 2. Check keyword / title alignment

Compare the resume's vocabulary and target title against the posting.

- The focus line should mirror the JD's job title.
- Areas of Expertise should use the posting's exact terms (where they truthfully apply).
- **Penalize**: zero or near-zero keyword overlap; title mismatch (resume targets a different/old role than the posting).

### 3. Require mirrored terminology ("translate, don't invent")

The resume should speak the JD's language, not synonyms the parser may miss.

- Prefer the posting's exact phrasing for skills and tools.
- Spell out acronyms on first use so both bots and humans match them.
- **Penalize**: candidate-invented terms where the JD uses a standard term; acronyms never expanded.

### 4. Front-load proof in the top third

The 6-second glance is won or lost in the top third of page one.

- Role match, aligned skills, and winning highlights must appear in the top third.
- Experience bullets should lead with results that map directly to key requirements.
- **Penalize**: top third that fails to signal role/skills/wins; duty-listing instead of outcomes.

### 5. Demand ATS-parseable formatting

Content must survive extraction field-by-field.

- Keep essentials (name, phone, email, city/ST, titles, employers, dates, education) in normal **body text**.
- Use **standard section labels**: Summary, Experience, Education, Skills / Areas of Expertise.
- Keep a consistent pattern: `Title – Employer, City, ST (Mon YYYY – Mon YYYY)`.
- Use simple bullets and common fonts (Calibri, Arial, Aptos, Times New Roman).
- Submit a `.docx` or `PDF`.
- **Penalize**: essential info in headers/footers, sidebars, text boxes, icons, columns, tables, or graphics; decorative elements competing with text; non-standard labels; wrong/odd file type; tiny fonts and dense paragraphs.

## What to Penalize (summary)

| Penalty trigger | Why it hurts | Stage affected |
|-----------------|--------------|----------------|
| Missing must-haves | Fails relevance scoring | 1 & 3 |
| Zero keyword overlap | ATS can't match; recruiter sees no fit | 1 & 2 |
| Title mismatch | Filtered by title/role; weak first glance | 1 & 2 |
| Unparseable layout (columns/tables/graphics/sidebars) | Data dropped or misread | 1 |
| Essentials in header/footer or text box | Fields not populated | 1 |
| Wrong file type | May not upload/parse cleanly | 1 |
| Keyword stuffing / invisible text | Backfires with humans; filtered by systems | 1 & 2 |
| Duties instead of results | No proof of impact; weak differentiation | 2 & 3 |
| Unsupported skill claims | No keyword proof in Experience | 3 |
| Acronyms never spelled out | Missed keyword matches | 1 |

## Scoring Guide

| Score band | What a jd-match looks like |
|------------|----------------------------|
| **High** | Most/all JD must-haves present and proven; focus line mirrors the title; Areas of Expertise reuse the posting's exact terms; top third signals role + skills + wins; results-first bullets map to key requirements; clean single-column body text, standard labels, consistent date pattern, `.docx`/PDF. Survives all 3 stages. |
| **Medium** | Partial must-have coverage; some keyword overlap but generic phrasing or invented terms; title roughly aligned; top third weakly signals fit; mostly parseable but minor layout risks (e.g., a table or unexpanded acronyms). Survives ATS and may make the "maybe" pile. |
| **Low** | Few/no must-haves covered; little to no keyword overlap; title mismatch; duty-listing without results; unparseable layout (columns, sidebars, graphics, essentials in headers/footers) or wrong file type; or keyword stuffing. Likely auto-rejected at Stage 1 or dropped at first glance. |

## Guidelines

- Score against *this* JD only — match is per-posting, not absolute.
- Weight must-have coverage and parseability highest; they are gate conditions.
- Credit truthful mirroring over volume of keywords.
- A visually clever but poorly organized resume scores low; so does keyword-stuffed but vague.

## Exceptions

- **Web-form applications**: when content is entered into fields (not uploaded), formatting matters less but exact titles/dates/employers and a value line still must mirror the resume.
- **Senior/decision-maker review**: once past screening, differentiation (CAR stories, relevance ordering) weighs more than raw keyword count.

## Quick Reference

| Rule | Summary |
|------|---------|
| Must-have coverage | Count JD must-haves present AND proven |
| Keyword/title alignment | Mirror the JD title; reuse exact terms |
| Mirrored terminology | Translate, don't invent; expand acronyms |
| Top-third proof | Lead with role, skills, wins, results |
| ATS-parseable format | Body text, standard labels, simple layout, .docx/PDF |
